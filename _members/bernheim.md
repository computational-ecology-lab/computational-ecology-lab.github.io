description: "I am a master's student coming from France. After completing my BSc in Ecology at the University of Strasbourg, I took a master’s degree in marine biology at the University of La Rochelle. I did several internships related to megafauna and biologging.


Since January 2026, I have been studying the movements of Atlantic gannets. My goal for this master's thesis is to be able to simulate the tracks of these long-lived seabirds during one of the most critical periods of their life, breeding. Using biologging data, I am first trying to find what triggers their turns and the distance between these turns, such as foraging state, wind direction, distance to the colony, and time of day. I might use this to parametrize an Agent-Based Model, which would simulate their overall trip.


I like to eat, climb, scuba dive, and debate about philosophy."
